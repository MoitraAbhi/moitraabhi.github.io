Bio

Abhishek is a fourth-year Electrical Engineering PhD student in Department of Electrical Engineering at Yale University, advised by Prof. Priyadarshini Panda.

My Research Interests lie in the co-search and co-design for in-memory computing and digital hardware accelerators for different machine learning tasks such as artificial neural networks, spiking neural networks and transformers.

Prior to joining Yale I recieved my BE in Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering degree from the Birla Institute of Technology and Science (BITS), Pilani (Goa). At BITS, I worked under Prof. Amalin Prince in designing FPGA-based hardware accelerators for signal processing applications. Meanwhile, we also interned at Western Digital where I worked towards developing a memory‑diagnostic feature for an in‑house tool. Later, I joined the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore under Prof. Chetan Singh Thakur to pursue my undergraduate thesis and research assistant-ship. At IISc, I focussed on designing FPGA-based hardware accelerators for binary neural networks and signal processing applications.
